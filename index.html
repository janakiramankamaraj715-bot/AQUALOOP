Interactive Filtration System

Visualize the multi-stage greywater treatment process from collection to reuse

Filtration Process Flow

Greywater Collection

Wastewater from sinks, showers, and laundry is collected through a dedicated plumbing diversion system.

Input

Mesh Filter

A stainless steel mesh screen removes large debris, hair, food particles, and fabric fibers.

Stage 1

Pebble Layer

Coarse pebbles (10-20mm) create channels for water flow and trap larger suspended solids.

Stage 2

Gravel Layer

Medium-grade gravel (5-10mm) provides secondary filtration, reducing turbidity by up to 40%.

Stage 3

Fine Sand Layer

Fine sand particles capture microscopic contaminants and further polish water clarity.

Stage 4

Activated Charcoal Layer

Activated carbon adsorbs chemicals, chlorine, odors, and organic compounds through microporous action.

Stage 5

Storage Tank

Treated water is stored in a UV-resistant sealed tank, maintaining quality for up to 48 hours.

Storage

Water Reuse

Recycled water is distributed for garden irrigation, toilet flushing, and outdoor cleaning tasks.

Output
